Industry benchmarks

What top companies do differently

The world's most productive organizations have specific, documented rules about meetings. Here's what the research and internal policies show.

AMZ

Amazon's two-pizza rule

Jeff Bezos mandates that any meeting should be small enough to be fed by two pizzas — typically 5–8 people. Larger groups are split or cancelled. Amazon also famously bans PowerPoint decks, replacing them with 6-page narrative memos read in silence at the start of every meeting.

GOOG

Google's 25/50-minute rule

Google defaults all meetings to 25 or 50 minutes rather than 30 or 60, building a natural buffer for restroom breaks and transition time. Internal research showed this reduced back-to-back meeting fatigue and increased overall focus time by over 15%.

META

Meta's "no meeting Wednesdays"

Meta implemented company-wide meeting-free Wednesdays to give engineers and makers uninterrupted deep work time. Studies show that even one day of protected time per week increases individual output by up to 30%.

ASNA

Asana's meeting-free mornings

Asana protects the first hours of every workday from meetings, citing research that the morning hours are when most knowledge workers report peak focus and creativity. Meetings are scheduled for afternoons only by default.
